>

>
> **Two value layers:**
> - **Layer 1 (the boring stuff):** Automate repetitive tasks, stop doing things twice, free up time. This is what gets people in the door.
> - **Layer 2 (the exciting stuff):** AI that finds clients, spots opportunities, monitors markets, surfaces insights. This is what makes them stay.
>
> **Tone:** Human, plain, specific. The pub test still applies. But this isn't a site for someone who doesn't know what a CRM is — it's for someone running a real business who's too busy to figure out where AI fits.

## How It Works

### Section Headline
How It Works

### Section Subheadline
Short engagements, clear deliverables, no open-ended consulting.

### Step 1 — Chat

We start with a conversation. You tell us what's eating your time, what frustrates you, and what you wish worked better. We'll ask some sharp questions about how your business actually runs. No pre-work, no questionnaires.

### Step 2 — Diagnosis

We look at the reality — the tools you use, the tasks you repeat, the things that fall through the cracks, and the opportunities sitting in plain sight. Then we tell you honestly what's worth doing and what isn't.

### Step 3 — Build

We set it up. Automations, AI tools, systems — whatever the diagnosis says will make the biggest difference. This isn't a strategy deck. You get working things, built and tested, typically within two to five weeks.

### Step 4 — Handover

We show you how everything works, make sure your team is confident with it, and hand it over. If you want lightweight support after that, we offer it — but the goal is always to give you something that runs without us.

## Tone Reference Card (for Claude Code)

When writing or refining any copy for this site, apply these tests:

1. **The pub test.** Would you say this to someone who runs a business, over a drink? If it sounds like a brochure, rewrite it.
2. **The "so what" test.** After every sentence, the reader should know what changes for them. Not what Intent does — what's different for the reader.
3. **The jargon alarm.** If a word wouldn't be used by a recruitment agency owner, a gym operator, or a property manager — find a simpler one.
4. **The trust test.** Every claim should feel honest and bounded. "We'll help" not "we'll transform." "Typically two to five weeks" not "rapid delivery." Specificity builds trust. Superlatives destroy it.
5. **The two-layer test.** Does the page communicate both layers? The boring stuff (automate, save time, stop repeating) AND the exciting stuff (find clients, spot opportunities, do new things)? If the site only sells automation, it's underselling Intent. If it only sells agents and AI magic, it'll feel like hype.

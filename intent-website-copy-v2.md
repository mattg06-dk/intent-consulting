# Intent Website — Draft Copy Reference (v2)

> **Audience reality:** For the first 6 months, Intent's actual clients are 1-5 person businesses where the founder does everything. The copy should speak to THAT person — not a 50-person company with a leadership team. The language should be human, plain, and specific. No consulting jargon, no AI hype.
>
> **Positioning:** The human consultancy that helps with AI. Lead with the person, not the technology.

## How It Works

### Section Headline Options

**Option A:** How It Works
**Option B:** Simple Process, Real Results
**Option C:** What Happens When You Work With Us

### Step 1 — Chat

We start with a conversation. You tell us what's taking up your time, what's frustrating you, and what you wish just worked better. No questionnaires, no pre-work — just a straight talk about your business.

### Step 2 — Diagnosis

We look at how your business actually runs — the tools you use, the tasks you repeat, the stuff that falls through the cracks. Then we tell you, honestly, what's worth fixing and what isn't.

### Step 3 — Build

We set up the automations and AI tools that will make the biggest difference. This isn't a strategy document — you get working systems, set up and tested, typically within two to five weeks.

### Step 4 — Handover

We show you how everything works, make sure you're comfortable with it, and hand it over. If something breaks or you want to add more later, we're a call away — but the goal is always to give you something that runs without us.

### Design Note
The tone of this section should feel like someone walking you through what'll happen — reassuring, not salesy. Think "here's what to expect" energy.

## Who It's For

### Section Headline Options

**Option A:** Is This You?
**Option B:** We Work With Small Businesses
**Option C:** You Don't Need a Big Team to Use AI

### Copy

You run a small business — maybe it's just you, maybe you've got a handful of people. You're good at what you do, but you're also doing the quoting, the invoicing, the follow-ups, the scheduling, and half a dozen other things that don't actually grow the business.

You've probably heard a lot about AI. Maybe you've played with ChatGPT. But you don't have time to figure out how to make it actually useful for your business — and you definitely don't want someone selling you something you don't need.

**That's where we come in.** We're not an AI company. We're a consultancy that uses AI as a tool — the same way a plumber uses a wrench. We figure out what's broken, fix it with the right tool, and make sure it keeps working.

### Qualifying Signals (optional checklist-style)

This might be a good fit if:

- You're spending more time running the business than doing the work you're actually good at
- You've got tasks you do the same way every week that feel like they could be automated
- You've looked at AI tools but aren't sure what's real and what's hype
- You want someone to just sort it out for you, not hand you a strategy deck
- You're a small team but you want to operate like a bigger one

## Tone Reference Card (for Claude Code)

When writing or refining any copy for this site, apply these tests:

1. **The pub test.** Would you say this to a friend who runs a small business, over a drink? If it sounds like a brochure, rewrite it.
2. **The "so what" test.** After every sentence, the reader should know what it means for them. Not what Intent does — what changes for the reader.
3. **The jargon alarm.** If a word wouldn't be used by a plumber, a personal trainer, or a coffee shop owner — find a simpler one. "Automation" is borderline acceptable. "Workflow orchestration" is not.
4. **The trust test.** Every claim should feel honest and bounded. "We'll help" not "we'll transform." "Typically two to five weeks" not "rapid delivery." Specificity builds trust. Superlatives destroy it.
